# Main Compliance Lead Agent Diagram Design

## Visual Philosophy: Deliberate Judgment

Deliberate Judgment expresses a compliance system as a carefully calibrated review table. Information moves from evidence intake into specialized analysis, then narrows into a single senior decision plane. The image should feel meticulously crafted, with the lead decision layer visibly heavier and more authoritative than the supporting agents.

The composition uses a restrained institutional palette: cool evidence blues, measured policy greens, amber domain review blocks, and a deeper ink tone for the senior lead agent. Conditional branches appear lighter so they read as supporting review work rather than parallel decision-makers.

Text remains sparse and operational. Labels name the exact implementation tasks and roles, while the hierarchy is carried by scale, spacing, and line weight. The final result should look like the product of painstaking systems design, with each connector and alignment placed by someone at the top of their field.

The subtle reference is a review room: four specialists submit memoranda, two optional reviewers attach supporting notes, and a senior reviewer signs the final risk judgment. The image should make that chain of responsibility clear at a glance.
